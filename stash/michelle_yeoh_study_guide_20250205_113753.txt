**Introduction**

Michelle Yeoh is a name synonymous with high-octane action and unforgettable performances in Hong Kong cinema. **Yes Madam (1985)**

Released in 1985, Yes Madam marked Michelle Yeoh's first major starring role. Directed by Corey Yuen, the film follows the story of two female police officers, Ng (Yeoh) and Carrie Morris (Cynthia Rothrock), who team up to take down a notorious crime lord. Yeoh's portrayal of Ng, a determined and resourceful inspector, set the tone for her future roles.

**Analysis of Ng's Character**

Ng is a product of a patriarchal society, where women are often underrepresented and overlooked. However, she defies these conventions and proves herself to be a capable and fearless leader. Through Ng's character, Yeoh showcases her impressive physical skills and athleticism, performing intricate fight choreography and stunts that would become a hallmark of her career.

**Fighting Style: Ng's Blending of Martial Arts and Combat**

Ng's fighting style is a unique blend of traditional martial arts and modern combat techniques. Yeoh's training in Kwok Tai Mime, a style that combines elements of judo and fasting-moving long-fist work, adds an extra layer of authenticity to Ng's movements. Her understanding of leverage and body mechanics allows her to deliver swift and devastating kicks and punches.

**Key Scenes and Impact: Showdowns and Double-Action Displays**

Yes Madam features two iconic showdowns: Ng's initial confrontation with Carrie Morris and the climactic battle against the crime lord's henchmen. The fight scenes demonstrate Yeoh's versatility and range, from lighting-fast hand movements to exquisite footwork. The famous "leg stretching" scene, where Ng takes on multiple opponents while balancing on one leg, showcases Yeoh's impressive balance and agility.
